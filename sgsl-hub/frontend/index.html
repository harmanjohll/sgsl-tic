How it works

Your hand movements are tracked in real-time using MediaPipe Hands. Each recording captures 21 hand landmarks per frame, encoding both shape and motion. These are sent to the Python backend where 59-dimensional feature vectors are extracted and stored. A machine-learning classifier (k-NN + DTW) is retrained after every new contribution.

About the recognition

Signs are recognized in two stages. Dynamic Time Warping (DTW) measures similarity between movement sequences, even at different speeds. A k-Nearest Neighbors (k-NN) classifier provides a second opinion trained on all contributed data. Both results are combined to give you the best match.
