How it works

Your movements are tracked in real-time using MediaPipe Holistic. Each recording captures both hands (21 landmarks each), face landmarks, and body pose — encoding hand shape, facial expression, and motion. These are sent to the Python backend where feature vectors are extracted and stored. A machine-learning classifier (k-NN + DTW) is retrained after every new contribution.

About the recognition

Signs are recognized in two stages. Dynamic Time Warping (DTW) measures similarity between movement sequences, even at different speeds. A k-Nearest Neighbors (k-NN) classifier provides a second opinion trained on all contributed data. Both results are combined to give you the best match.
