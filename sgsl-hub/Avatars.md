The development of virtual avatars capable of producing linguistically accurate and biomechanically natural sign language requires a departure from traditional computer graphics approaches. Sign language is a multi-modal system where meaning is conveyed through the high-speed coordination of the upper body, facial expressions, and extremely intricate hand articulations. To achieve realism, an avatar must be grounded in the same biomechanical constraints that govern human movement, utilizing expressive body templates like SMPL-X that provide a unified representation of the body, hands, and face.Anatomical Foundations and Skeletal HierarchyThe skeletal structure must be modeled as a hierarchy of kinematical chains. For sign language, a Level of Articulation (LOA) of 4 is the minimum standard, as it include the necessary anatomical details of the hands. The hierarchy begins at the humanoid_root (pelvis) and extends through the spinal column to the radiocarpal joints (wrists).The 22-DoF Hand ModelTo precisely calculate fingertip positions—essential for signs involving contact—the avatar utilizes the Denavit-Hartenberg (D-H) convention. Storage and NormalizationLandmarks are stored as 3D coordinate data $(x, y, z)$ in JSON or database structures. Before animation, these points must be normalized relative to frame dimensions to ensure the data is independent of the camera's absolute position or the signer's size.3. Retargeting and Inverse Kinematics (IK)Since MediaPipe's skeleton differs from the avatar's (SMPL-X), a "General Motion Retargeting" (GMR) pipeline is required. This involves:Differential IK Solver: Solving for the vector of revolute joint angles $\theta$ that minimizes the distance between the avatar's joint positions and the target landmarks $p_{target}$.Smoothing: A temporal filter cascade or "Motion Blending" is applied to remove noise from the 2D/3D tracking, ensuring the avatar does not "jitter" during high-speed signs.Technical Directive: Code Implementation PromptTask: Develop a data-driven 3D avatar system for Sign Language Production.Implementation Specification:Skeletal Core: Implement an SMPL-X body template with a LOA-4 H-Anim hierarchy. For target points in the outer signing squares, initiate a torso-first movement followed by synchronized arm/hand articulation.Facial Blendshapes: Map MediaPipe face landmarks to SMPL-X expression parameters ($\psi$). Prioritize brow-furrowing/raising and lip-morphemes (oo, cha, puffed cheeks) for grammatical accuracy.Smoothness and Skinning: Use Minimum-Jerk Trajectory interpolation (5th-order polynomials) for all joint transitions to ensure bell-shaped velocity profiles.
